# MattaNutra Update - 8 May 2026

Today moved MattaNutra forward on both the marketing engine and the core HealthScore conversion flow.

## 1. Blog Platform Added

We added a database-driven blog section to the site.

What this enables:

- MattaNutra can publish useful wellness and supplement-related content.
- Blog content can sit under the main site and direct readers into the HealthScore assessment.
- Blog posts are managed from the backend rather than hardcoded pages.
- OpenClaw or another external content agent can create, update, publish, and manage articles through protected admin endpoints.

Business value:

- The blog becomes the top of the marketing funnel.
- Articles can support organic search, social distribution, and trust-building.
- Each article can be used to move readers toward the assessment.

## 2. Testimonials Added

We added testimonials in the same database-driven way as the blog.

What this enables:

- Testimonials can be stored, updated, and reused across the site.
- Testimonials can include images, with graceful fallback handling when no image is available.
- External systems such as OpenClaw can manage testimonials through protected admin endpoints.

Business value:

- Testimonials give the site social proof.
- They can be matched to different funnel moments: assessment start, HealthScore page, free email exit page, blog pages, and future payment pages.
- This gives the business a flexible proof layer without code changes for every new testimonial.

## 3. HealthScore Formula Implemented

We implemented Ian's HealthScore formula from the provided workbook.

What changed:

- The HealthScore now follows the supplied spreadsheet logic.
- It is generated on the backend as the single source of truth.
- The frontend no longer generates its own independent score.
- The score is based on the six HealthScore domains and the workbook scoring structure.
- The implementation was checked against sample workbook outcomes.

Business value:

- The HealthScore is no longer a decorative quiz result.
- It is now aligned to the business scoring model.
- This creates a more credible conversion moment before the plan choice.
- Future reassessments can compare like-for-like score movement.

## 4. HealthScore Page Improved

We improved the HealthScore page visually and commercially.

What changed:

- The score presentation was refined.
- The six-domain snapshot was improved.
- A radar-style visualisation was added for the domain pattern.
- The AI-generated overview was simplified into one statement.
- The overview now sits inside the main HealthScore panel as a clean white block with an importance icon.

Business value:

- The HealthScore page now feels more like a meaningful product moment.
- It helps the user understand why the next step matters.
- It gives the plan gate more persuasive context.

## 5. Free Email Path Made Live

We made the free email option live for users who are not ready to select a paid plan.

What this enables:

- A user can request a free 3-point nutrition plan by email.
- The backend processes the request.
- The email is sent through the configured SMTP service.
- The system can include a recurring 60-day reassessment reminder.
- Unsubscribe handling is in place.
- Email activity is audited.

Business value:

- Skeptical users no longer have to convert immediately or disappear.
- The free path captures leads.
- It creates a reason to continue the relationship.
- The 60-day reassessment gives the business a natural return loop.

## 6. Dynamic Personalised Marketing Added

We added AI-personalised marketing copy to the HealthScore page and plan gate.

What changed:

- The backend asks Grok to generate a concise HealthScore overview.
- The fixed title "Your HealthScore is ready" stays stable.
- Supporting plan-gate copy can adapt to the user's profile.
- The plan-gate eyebrow can adapt.
- The plan-gate subtitle can adapt.
- Three feature cards are generated dynamically in English and Thai.
- The AI output is validated and retried if it does not match the required structure.
- Static fallback copy remains in place if AI output is unavailable.

Business value:

- The sales moment can feel specific to the user rather than generic.
- The plan choice can emphasize the benefits most relevant to the user's HealthScore and answers.
- This should improve the bridge from free HealthScore to paid plan selection.

What they cover:

## Current Position

MattaNutra now has the core shape of a conversion engine:

1. Content attracts visitors.
2. The assessment captures structured wellness data.
3. The backend creates a workbook-aligned HealthScore.
4. The HealthScore page builds trust and personal relevance.
5. The user can choose free email, Precision, or Pro.
6. Free users can be captured and followed up.
7. Paid users can move toward a full formulation flow.

## Main Remaining Gaps

- Payment still needs to be activated.
- Product matching and affiliate purchase logic need to mature.
- Pro advisor handoff needs to become real and reliable.
- LINE, WhatsApp, and Telegram plan handoff should be tightened.
- Funnel analytics should be added so the business can optimize conversion.
- Safety and sanity checks should be strengthened before larger traffic.

## Summary

Today shifted the product from "assessment and formulation prototype" toward a real commercial funnel. The most important progress was connecting content, HealthScore credibility, free lead capture, and personalised marketing into one coherent customer journey.
